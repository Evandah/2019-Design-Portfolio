Starship Suicide Prevention

The Challenge

New Zealand has the highest Youth Suicide rate in the world. Our Challenge was to uncover the main barriers preventing young people reaching out for support, and designing a solution that helps them overcome these challenges. The aim was to deliver an app that school councilors, and other therapists could recommend to their patients.

The process

  • 6 week discovery phase
  • Brand identity + design system
  • Mobile app UI/UX design
  • 14 week agile development

User needs to future vision

Over 6 weeks I collaborated with a cross-disciplinary team of stakeholders including youth councilors, Child Psychiatrists, Youth leaders, Parents, plus the Datacom innovation and design team.

Our strategy focused on understanding the main pain points young people encounter during moments of depression, and co-designing a tool they felt would help. From insights, we formed the vision statement “Helping young people get the “right” support from their family and friends”.

Prototyping a new experience

Designing, prototyping and user testing in rapid cycles during the discovery phase allowed us to validate a range of different solutions young people felt would help. For example, we prototyped a fun gamified journaling tool that helped young people easily articulate their feelings in a message they could send to their family and friends.

Having the customers and stakeholder voice in the same room allowed us to refine our experiments in real-time to make the right trade-offs between customer desirability, and business constraints. It also exposed the client to the value of a lean product-design approach and helped obtain buy-in as we pivoted to three different design directions.

Validating the prototype

The end product was a messaging platform that helps family and friends easily articulate what type of support they need from each other to move past the “lows”. We were able to validate the product with young people before any code was written. The highlight of the project was hearing a young girl say “This would help me and my older brother so much” during user testing.

Translating design into dev requirements

As we moved into the build phase we adopted Agile and Scrum methodologies. As a supporting UI designer this meant I had to prepare many assets for development, provide in-depth user flows for testing, as well as go the extra mile and prepare documentation with 30 different development requirement stories so the development team had a single source of truth they could refer to in times of need.
